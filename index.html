The Concept I Introduced
⭐ Original Framework
Cognitive Delegation Threshold
CDT — in development
Human–AI Cognitive Interaction — Defining When Learners Should Think, and When AI Should.
"The point at which a learner transfers cognitive work — reasoning, evaluation, synthesis — to AI.
Understanding this threshold is essential for AI-integrated education that preserves human agency and epistemic responsibility."
Why it matters
→As AI embeds into classrooms, knowing when to rely on it is as critical as knowing how.
→Uncalibrated delegation risks replacing deep thinking with AI dependency.
→CDT gives educators a framework to design AI that strengthens — not replaces — human cognition.
01
Preserves deep learning
Identifies when AI delegation forfeits productive struggle.
02
Prevents over-reliance
Distinguishes scaffolding from displacement.
03
Guides AI curriculum design
A practical tool for responsible AI integration.
04
Informs learning policy
Evidence-anchored lens for evaluating AI-first education.
